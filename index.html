About

A quick intro—so you know who you’re rocking with.

I’m Dr. Rafael Matos—born in Puerto Rico to Dominican parents, raised in the South Bronx, and now proudly calling Chicago home. And yes… I’m loving life in the Windy City.

When I’m not teaching, researching, creating, or speaking, you can catch me repping my teams: Chicago Bulls, New York Yankees, and the USC Trojans (FIGHT ON!).

I love volunteering and I have a real passion for mentoring young people—because talent is everywhere, but opportunity and guidance? Not always.

Cultural Systems Strategist

Research energy, but practical. Fun. Built to be used.

I earned my PhD in Communication Media and Instructional Technology from Indiana University of Pennsylvania—a public institution near Pittsburgh, PA (GO CRIMSON HAWKS!).

My work lives at the intersection of people, culture, technology, corporate communication, and storytelling. I study how meaning moves through systems—brands, communities, workplaces, media—then turns into behavior.

Right now, I’m exploring 1980s toy IP and its influence on modern storytelling ecosystems— the kind of world-building that turns a product into a universe, and an audience into a community.
